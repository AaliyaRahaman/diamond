The Diamond

What is a Diamond?

Diamonds are naturally occurring crystals of carbon. Formed under intense heat and pressure deep beneath the Earth's surface, they are the hardest natural material known.

Formation & Structure

The formation of diamonds requires extremely high pressure and temperature conditions. Most natural diamonds are formed at depths of 140 to 190 kilometers in the Earth's mantle.

Interesting Uses

While diamonds are widely known for their use in jewelry, they also play a critical role in industries—from cutting and drilling tools to electronics.

Quick Fact

Diamonds can burn at about 850°C. Yes, the hardest thing on Earth is not fireproof.
